Use Case Example

Example of a Brief Use Case

UC1: Purchase Ticket

A customer arrives at Ticket Machine and selects the zone he wants to
travel to. Ticket Machine displays price for traveling to that zone.
As the customer enters coins the machine reduces the amount to enter 
(shown on display) by the coin value, until it reaches zero. 
Then the Ticket Machine creates and dispenses a ticket with the fair
value, and returns any change.

Structure of a Fully Dressed Use Case

This is the Use Case template suggested by Alistair Cockburn and used in the textbook. Other templates and examples are on the class home page.

UC1: Purchase Ticket
Customer purchases a ticket at Skytrain ticket machine.
Scope
Skytrain ticket machine
Level
user goal (other levels are: subfunction, component, system)
Primary Actor
Customer (who requests services from the software?)
Stakeholders and their interests
this section is sometimes called "secondary actors" or "other actors"
Customer wants fast, reliable purchase of ticket
Management wants reliable system, recording of transactions with totals
Preconditions
don't list obvious things
No transaction is in-process at the ticket machine. Ticket machine has adequate supply of coins and tickets.
Main Success Scenario
  1. Customer selects zone.
  2. System displays ticket price.
  3. Customer inserts coin.
  4. System deducts coin value from displayed price and updates display.
  5. Repeat steps 3-4 until value of coins equals or exceeds ticket price.
  6. System enters ticket value and current date/time onto ticket.
  7. System issues ticket and any change to customer.
  8. Customer takes ticket and change.
Alternative Flows or Extensions
3a1. Customer cancels transaction.
3a2. Ticket machine refunds coin(s) entered by customer.
3b1. Customer selects a different zone
3b2. Ticket price is recomputed and new price is shown.
Open Issues
1. Can customer select a different zone after inserting some coins?
Business Rules
reference to any business rules. The business rules are in a separate document; don't repeat them here.
