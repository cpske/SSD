Olympics Scorekeeper

The Olympics Gymnastics consists of events or competitions, such as "Parallel bars". Each event has one or more rounds. In each round, the contestants perform and are evaluated by a panel of judges.

After each contestant's performance (in a single round), the judges each award a score to the performance; the score is a number -- usually a floating point number between 0 and 10 with one decimal digit, such as 8.7. The judges' scores are combined to get a single score for the contestant's performance.
How the judges scores are combined may vary: in some events the judges' scores are simply averaged (often the highest and lowest scores are discarded and the rest are averaged). In other events the judges scores are averaged and then multiplied by a difficulty factor for the contestant's performance (e.g. a "triple flip" may have a difficulty factor of 3.2).

If the event has more than one round, then the contestant's final score is the sum of his scores from each round. The winner of the event is the contestant with the highest total score.

  1. Construct a domain model for the olympics gymnastics. The model should be able to handle multiple events.
     
  2. Assign these responsibilities to classes:
    • assign score (judge awards a score to a performance)
    • know all the scores for a contestant -- know the individual scores from each judge for each round
    • compute a contestant's overall score for a single performance
    • compute a contestant's total score (total of all performances)
    • rank the contestants and compute the winner
    • know the judges -- how do we get the judges for a competition?
    • know the contestants -- who are the contestants in a competition?

     
  3. Name as least one more responsibility. You can include creation responsibilities.
